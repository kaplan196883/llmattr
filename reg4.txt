## `exp_long_v2` — modified experiment spec

### 3. Experimental matrix

Run four main conditions.

#### Condition A — baseline long run

This is the direct extension of `exp_long`.

* steps per run: **40**
* prompt families: **3**
* initial conditions per family: **3**
* runs per IC: **5** instead of 3
* temperature: **0.8**
* max output tokens: **120**
* clip: **12,000 chars**

Reason: your current report already argues that 15 steps is too short to distinguish basin entry from in-basin recurrence. 

#### Condition B — warmer exploration

Same as A, except:

* temperature: **1.1**

Reason: your report explicitly notes that warmer temperatures may expose recurrence inside the basin. 

#### Condition C — cooler collapse

Same as A, except:

* temperature: **0.4**

Reason: this gives a contrast condition. If recurrence appears only at higher temperature, that supports the interpretation that the system needs exploration to move within the basin rather than collapsing straight to a fixed point.

#### Condition D — memory stress

Same as A, except:

* max output tokens: **180**
* clip window: **4,000–6,000 chars**

Reason: in `exp_default`, clipping barely mattered, so you did not really probe bounded-memory dynamics yet. This condition forces overwrite pressure and tests whether basin geometry survives stronger memory competition.

### 8. New metrics

Keep the current three:

* recurrence
* dwell
* basin

But replace “recurrence” with **three recurrence-style metrics**.

#### R1. Global recurrence

Your current metric:
[
|z_t-z_s| < \varepsilon,\quad |t-s|>\tau
]

Keep it, but demote it to a coarse screen.

#### R2. Late-time recurrence

Compute recurrence only on:
[
t,s \ge 0.7T
]

This directly tests whether the trajectory revisits neighborhoods **after** basin entry, instead of mixing drift-to-basin with in-basin motion.

#### R3. Exit-and-return rate

Define the dominant late-time cluster (C^*). Then measure:

* number of exits from (C^*)
* probability of re-entry after exit
* mean time to re-entry

This is the most useful new recurrence metric.

Why this change:
Your current report explains exactly why the global recurrence metric can be lower in the recursive trajectory than in its shuffled twin: smooth drift plus a lag filter suppresses near-time close pairs.

* basin-entry distribution by condition
* recurrence metrics only after basin entry

This directly operationalizes your own interpretation that the first ~10 steps may be spent reaching the basin.

### 15. The exact interpretation logic

If you see:

* strong basin
* strong dwell
* weak late-time recurrence
* low re-entry after exit

then the system is behaving like:

> **fixed-point or fixed-region attractors**

If you see:

* strong basin
* strong dwell
* late-time recurrence above shuffled null
* repeated exit and re-entry

then the system is behaving more like:

> **orbiting or metastable attractors**

That is the main distinction your current experiment cannot yet resolve cleanly.
